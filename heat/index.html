India Humid Heat Monitor

Public heat communication in India runs mostly on dry-bulb air temperature. This dashboard shows which cities look less dangerous on that ordinary ranking but become high-risk once humidity, radiant heat, and wind are accounted for.

Rank-shift: dry-bulb vs. humid-heat

Each line is one city. Left: rank by today's peak dry-bulb temperature. Right: rank by today's peak estimated WBGT. Highlighted lines are the ten biggest climbers — cities that look unremarkable on ordinary temperature but rank far more dangerous once humidity and radiant heat are counted.

Workday clock

Hourly WBGT risk bands, including evening and night hours — coming next. Humid heat often stays risky well after sunset as relative humidity rises, which challenges "avoid only the afternoon" guidance.

Estimated WBGT and wet-bulb temperature are model outputs from weather-station-grade data (Open-Meteo), not physical measurements. Language on this site describes "lower-risk outdoor work windows under estimated WBGT assumptions" — never "safe hours." Full methodology, validity ranges, and an AI-transparency note are on the methods page.
